Cybersecurity and Privacy

1. What does it do?


It is undeniable that privacy had been one of the most vital issues among people, businesses, and organizations even before the “digital big bang”. However, since the early days after the revolutionary invention of the computer and the arrival of the Internet, information security had become more important but more vulnerable and fragile since the information was transmitted to a digital platform instead of being in physical shape like before. Therefore, the term “Cybersecurity” had arrived, and it became the key to protecting and maintaining privacy, users’ assets, and classified information. In 2020, V. Michael and B. Ian noted in their book that cybersecurity covers a wide range of social and technical problems that must be taken into consideration to secure networked information systems and network privacy [1]. In addition, in 2021, according to A. Moallem, “Cybersecurity is a growing field of science composed of very diverse types of technologies used to protect systems from different kinds of attacks, some known and some unknown. Due to the complexity of the evolving aspects of these areas, there is no standard classification of protective technologies.” [2, p. 1].

The traditional approach of security to protect computer systems and network systems like authentication devices, firewalls, and virtual private networks (VPN) has a lot of different kinds of vulnerabilities since cyberattacks nowadays have become more advanced and sophisticated and they can disrupt security programs and infiltrate systems [3]. In the upcoming years, it is critical and escalating compulsory for companies and organizations to deal with complexity since the sprawling increase of today’s network scale is inevitable and clear borders, access points will be eliminated; IT teams can reach the point of exhaustion from relentless sprint of protecting information; more importantly, the volume of data will rise dramatically, which can give cybercriminals more thing to exploit, and “complexity is the enemy of security” [4]. Therefore, different technologies must be applied and integrated to upgrade cybersecurity so that it can resist modern cyberattacks.

Nowadays, as technology is growing rapidly, many existing tools and technologies can be used to enhance cybersecurity. There are two ways of categorizing cybersecurity technologies. The first way is based on the components that each technology is supposed to protect which are network, application, endpoint, data, identity, database, infrastructure, mobile, cloud, and backup [2, p.25]. The second way is the solution-based classification including Identity and Access Management, Risk and Compliance Management, Encryption, Data Loss Prevention (DLP), Unified Threat Management (UTM), Firewall, Antivirus/ Anti-malware Solutions, Intrusion Detection System (IDS)/ Intrusion Prevention System (IPS), Disaster Recovery, Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) Mitigation and Web filtering [2, p. 25].

Moreover, some specific technologies can be optimized for information protection. Firstly, data encryption is a very powerful way to protect confidential information from hackers. Moreover, this technology uses a symmetric-key algorithm and asymmetric-key algorithm such as Blowfish and RSA, respectively, to encode the sensitive data to meaningless language and this encoded data is only readable by the person having the decryption key [2, p. 30]. Secondly, biometric technology has developed significantly since it uses physical and behavioral traits which are permanent and unique for authentication, surveillance, and identification instead of conventional passwords, which is a very safe and reliable approach [2, p. 50]. Thirdly, firewall technology has been widely implemented in hardware and software from personal devices to large data systems in companies and organizations. In detail, a firewall is a network security system designed to monitor traffic to and from a computer or computer network and to block traffic that does not comply with security parameters in place so that it can protect a computer or computer network from cyberattacks [5]. In addition, malware protection technology is indispensable due to the proliferation of smart technologies and malware can be a significant threat. For instance, there were a lot of infamous malware attacks in the past like Stuxnet Worm (2010), Backoff (2014), or WannaCry Ransomware (2017), etc [2, p. 102-103]. Therefore, anti-malware solutions must be constantly conducted to detect and prevent malware from stealing confidential information. Finally, as we are living in a world of expanding connectivity, the Internet of Things technology will be a great cybersecurity approach. Moreover, IoT technologies connect all devices and make them “smart” by connecting them to other devices in a network and “intelligent” by using machine learning and data science to manage and analyze real data to make decisions and predict future events [2, p. 116]. In the future, as newer technologies will be introduced and developed, cybersecurity will implement those latest technologies to protect data and information.

When a person wants to make change to a block, they will have to create another the block which will stored all their modified data. Therefore, the old data will stay untouched.

However, after filling in the form on the university website, they have to wait for a period of time for the confirmation email on Outlook depending on their network connection, so it can take a long time if their connection is weak, which can be very uncomfortable and annoying. Students, lecturers, and visitors do not have to complete the form on the university website because they can fill in it and instantly receive the confirmation email in the RMIT SGS app. Since the security officers have to check the QR code of the Health Declaration Form, we have also replicated the form and the confirmation email like the former version on the university website so that people will not be confused” [2, pp. 27-28].

In addition, the RMIT SGS application also has some extra features. Firstly, there will be multilingual support for the vast majority of users, our application content can be switched between Vietnamese, English, Chinese, Japanese, and Korean since these are popular languages and RMIT University is an international environment, so there are a lot of people from various countries and different cultures. Secondly, cross-platform support will also be available on the RMIT SGS app. Therefore, Apple, Android, and Windows-based devices can get access to our application. Additionally, the campus virtual map and route will be constantly updated since there will be some changes to the campus in the future and there will be a downloadable campus map for offline usage.

Scope and Limits

RMIT SGS aims for one for all utility, which includes navigation around the school, daily Health Declaration form, and social dashboard. Our navigation function operate similar to Google Maps, or like the navigation in the Disney World for their parks, the navigation will show the routes and time estimate arrival of each buildings and rooms, also shows cafeterias, convenience stores, and libraries, which you can easily click to go there quickly or save to a preferred list. With the Covid-19 pandemic still surging in numbers every single day, and RMIT dedication to return back to campus with high precaution. Daily Health Declaration form is a necessity for student and faculty members to traverse around campus with clearance. The current method is a link to a Microsoft Office form using a web browser, our idea is to make the form integrate into the app and have the QR code for confirmation instantly, which is more time efficient and less complicated. Our third feature is a social dashboard, it’s a news and event locator that is currently happening around RMIT, including your classes calendar when you login.

All the features mentioned is all of our core point of this project, however, given the time to show the proof of concept, we will focus on making the navigation feature work properly and the daily Health Declaration form as our main point, the social dashboard can as a later-on feature because it not necessary as the other two with the current situation. Also our plan of having multiple languages selection, for the time being it will only have English and Vietnamese based on the majority of students and faculty members.

Testing

Our testing procedure will be divided into two phase:

Phase 1:

In Phase 1, our prototype will be tested by members in the team. The procedures will be: predicting target user’s needs, setting objectives, testing, fix and debug code. The procedures will be repeated until the app satisfies all the objectives. To be specific, each member of the team will think of what they will need from a navigation app as an RMIT student. After that, those needs will be listed out as the objectives that the app will aim to achieve. Then the app will be tested to see if it has satisfied the objectives. If there are any flaws that we find, we will have a discussion to see if any change is needed. Then the coding team will have to fix the problem or add-in some functionality for our app, we may even have to restructure our code if there are some major bugs or mistakes made in our code.

Phase 2:

Phase 2 of our testing procedure will be the public beta test. We are going to recruit some volunteers to be the testers of our product. The reason we chose to do this is because even though we have tested everything by ourselves, there may still be some flaws that we have not noticed. Furthermore, the opinions sometimes are biased as we will have the mindset of a developer of that app when we are testing it. Therefore, the outsider, which is also the main target of ours, will be the perfect choice for the beta testing. The procedure of phase 2 will be: Testing, feedback, analyze, fixing. First, the volunteers will be handed our product and get to try, experience it for a period of time, then they will provide feedback of their thoughts about the product. The analyst of our team will analyze the feedback, stating out the problem we are facing and all the flaws that the testers have found. Finally, the coding team will fix the problems so that the outcome will be the best version of the app. Similar to Phase 1, the whole procedure will be repeated until there are no flaws left in our product.
